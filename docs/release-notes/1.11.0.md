# v1.11.0

Monize now speaks your language. The headline of this release is full **internationalization** — the entire interface, plus emails and error messages, is translated into **nine languages**. It also brings a personalised look with **fifteen colour themes**, a one-click **Auto-Merge Payees** tool to clean up duplicate payees, **financial-institution** grouping for your accounts, two brand-new reports (**Credit Utilization** and **Monthly Breakdown**), a rebuilt mobile menu, and truer foreign-currency valuations for your portfolio.

## Languages

### Monize now speaks your language

The Monize interface is now fully translated into **nine languages**: English, German (Deutsch), Spanish (Español), French (Français), Italian (Italiano), Dutch (Nederlands), Polish (Polski), Portuguese (Português), and Brazilian Portuguese (Português do Brasil).

- **Pick your language in Settings → Preferences.** It's saved to your profile, so it follows you to every device you sign in from.
- **Not signed in yet?** The login, registration, and password-reset screens now have their own language switcher, and Monize makes a sensible first guess from your browser's language when you create an account.
- **It's not just buttons and labels.** Validation messages, toasts, and the action-history log are all translated too.
- **Emails arrive in your language.** Password resets, emergency-access notices, and every other email are sent in the language stored on your account — not whatever language the server happens to run in.
- **Numbers, currencies, and dates** are formatted to match each language's conventions.

## Personalisation

### Fifteen colour themes

Monize now ships with **fifteen colour themes** you can switch between in **Settings → Preferences → Colour theme**: Default, Latte, MS Money (a nod to the classic Microsoft Money look), Newspaper, Burgundy, Nord, Forest, Solarized, Gruvbox, Dracula, Tokyo Night, Rosé Pine, Midnight, High contrast, and Colour-blind safe.

- Each theme works in **both light and dark mode** — your existing light / dark / system setting still chooses which variant you see.
- Themes **apply instantly** and **save on their own**, with no separate "Save" step.

## Payees

### Auto-Merge Payees: clean up duplicates in one pass

Imports tend to leave you with near-duplicate payees — "LIDL", "LIDL sp. z o.o.", "LIDL WARSZAWA 0421" all pointing at the same shop. The new **Auto-Merge Payees** tool finds these look-alikes automatically with fuzzy matching and groups them so you can collapse each cluster into a single payee.

- Tune how aggressive the matching is with a **similarity threshold** and a **minimum group size**.
- **Ignore common words** stops generic terms (Bank, Store, country names, and the like) from gluing unrelated payees together.
- Optionally **require a matching category**, so payees only merge when they truly belong together.
- **Review every group before applying:** rename the surviving payee, set an alias pattern so future imports are captured automatically, and tick or untick individual payees — or whole groups — with select / deselect-all.

## Accounts

### Group your accounts by financial institution

A new **Institutions** page lets you record the banks, brokerages, and credit unions behind your accounts. Give each one a name, website, and country, and Monize can fetch its logo. Then link your accounts to their institution and see, at a glance, how many accounts each one holds.

- Search and sort your institutions, and filter by whether their accounts are active or closed.
- A **Manage Accounts** panel lets you add or remove the accounts tied to an institution without leaving the page.
- The account info widget surfaces the institution alongside each account.

## Reports

### Credit Utilization report

A new report shows how much of your available credit you're actually using. A **total-utilization donut chart** breaks down drawn-versus-available credit, colour-coded by risk (green under 30%, amber in between, red over 75%), backed by a per-card bar chart and a sortable table of credit limit, balance used, amount available, and utilisation for each card or line of credit. Closed accounts and accounts with no credit limit are left out.

### Monthly Breakdown report

A month-by-month grid of spending and income organised by category, with running **totals and per-month averages**. Cells are **highlighted when a month strays from its average**, so unusual months jump out. Click any **category to drill straight into the matching transactions** — click a single month's cell to see just that month. **Toggle between amounts and percentage-of-month**, and **export the whole grid to CSV**.

## Reconciliation

### Pay down your card right after reconciling

When you finish reconciling a credit card or line of credit, Monize now offers to **set up — or update — the scheduled payment** that pays it off, pre-filled with the balance you just reconciled. One click takes you to the bill with the amount already filled in; you just confirm the date and frequency. (Loans and mortgages, which have fixed payments, are left alone.)

## Mobile & Navigation

### A new mobile menu

The mobile side menu has been rebuilt into a dedicated slide-out drawer. Along the way, the Portfolio Value chart gets tighter margins on small screens, and numeric columns across the app now line up cleanly thanks to a global tabular-figures rule.

## Investments

### Truer foreign-currency valuations

- **Portfolio summaries now value your holdings using live FX rates**, so a multi-currency portfolio reflects today's exchange rates rather than a stale conversion.
- **Intraday chart bars fall back to daily FX history** when an intraday rate isn't available, so foreign-currency holdings no longer leave gaps in the graph.
- Administrators get a **force-update endpoint** to refresh historical security prices on demand.

## Bug Fixes

- The transaction list now **shows the linked payee's name** when a row had no stored payee text, instead of leaving it blank. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- Restoring a backup now **clears the action-history log**, so Undo can't roll you back to data from before the restore.

## Under the Hood

- **Hardened CI/CD pipeline** — added security, quality, and observability checks, moved image publishing to a manual-only release workflow, and resolved a cache-poisoning finding flagged by static analysis.
- **More safety nets in the test suite** — comprehensive cross-user isolation tests and a full backup/restore round-trip test.
- The **OAuth/OIDC consent flow** now grants all missing scopes in a single step.
- **MCP requests at the root URL** now route correctly to the backend MCP endpoint.
- **Release tooling:** the CI pipeline gained flexible version bumping and release-notes support, and the historical release notes (v1.9.4 through v1.10.5) were backfilled into the repo.
- Routine dependency updates.
