# v1.12.0

This is a big one, and the headline is **loans and mortgages**: every loan now gets a full **detail page** with an amortization schedule, an interactive **overpayment simulator** with saved what-if scenarios, an editable **interest-rate history**, and smart **overpayment recognition** that understands how your bank actually books payments. The dashboard becomes **fully customizable** — reorder, hide, and configure more than a dozen widgets — and **every account type gets its own detail page**. Investors get an **all-securities performance chart**, **per-security portfolio breakdowns**, **geographic look-through**, and flexible **KEY:VALUE tags**. Transaction search now understands **amounts and dates in your own format**, AI insights arrive **in your language** with clickable links, and default categories and all emails are **fully localized**.

## Loans & Mortgages

### A dedicated detail page for every loan

Loans and mortgages now have a full **detail page**: summary cards, a month-grouped **Loan Schedule** with real per-row interest, and an interactive **overpayment simulator**. Model recurring extra payments or one-off lump sums and see exactly how they change your payoff date and total interest — then **save scenarios** and compare strategies side by side. A **Past Impact** analysis compares your actual payment history against the original contractual schedule, so you can see how much your past overpayments have already saved you. The schedule, rate history, and scenario comparison all **export to CSV**, and the payoff comparison chart exports to **PNG**.

- Two overpayment modes: **shorten the term** (keep the installment, finish sooner) or **lower the installment** (keep the end date, pay less each period).
- The engine supports all payment frequencies and Canadian semi-annual compounding, and now powers the Loan Amortization and Debt Payoff Timeline reports too, so every projection in the app agrees.

### Interest-rate change history

Variable-rate loan? Monize now keeps a **rate-change timeline** per loan or mortgage. Record rate resets, term renewals, and refinances manually — or let Monize **auto-detect historical rate changes from your payment history**, including loans where the bank books interest separately from principal. Rows are badged as manual, inferred, or initial; you can edit them right in the schedule; and detection is **non-destructive** — your account's rate and payment are yours, set only in the edit form, never silently overwritten. When a rate change affects a linked scheduled payment, Monize shows you a **before/after preview and asks permission** before touching it.

### Overpayment recognition that matches your bank

Tag extra principal payments with a designated **category, payee, or memo text** (any one match counts) and Monize recognizes them as 100% principal in the schedule and simulator. Interest recognition is now universal across booking styles — split transactions, separately-booked interest expenses, even ledgers that tag the principal transfer and interest expense with the same category — with a per-loan **interest booking mode** (Auto / Split / Separate) if you want to be explicit. Interest-only grace periods show up as their own rows. And if you're not sure how to record loan payments in the first place, a collapsible **"How to record loan payments"** guide is right in the form.

## Dashboard

### Make the dashboard yours

A new **Customize** control lets you **show, hide, and reorder every dashboard widget** — drag and drop or use the arrow buttons — with your layout saved to your profile so it follows you across devices. Widgets can be given **custom names and descriptions**, the customize modal works comfortably on mobile, and a new **Favourite Reports** widget puts your starred built-in, custom, and investment reports one click away.

### A dozen new configurable widgets

The dashboard gains **11 new opt-in widgets**: Portfolio Value, Spending by Payee, Monthly Spending Trend, Income by Source, Sector Weightings, Geographic Allocation, Security Type Allocation, Weekend vs. Weekday, Recurring Expenses, and Credit Utilization (total and by account). Each widget has its own gear menu for **time range, account filters, and chart type**, settings persist per widget and sync across devices, and every widget wears a clear time-range label. The existing Income vs Expenses and Expenses by Category widgets got the same treatment, and the Portfolio Value widget picked up a **live price refresh** button.

## Accounts

### A detail page for every account type

Click into any account and you now land on a **type-specific detail view** instead of a generic list:

- **Banking**: balance history, recurring charges (with one-click conversion to scheduled bills), a cash-flow mini-report, top spending categories, and a **90-day balance forecast**.
- **Credit cards**: statement-cycle information, spending breakdown by category — including **late-posting, unreconciled charges from the previous cycle**, so the amount owed is right — plus interest and fees tracking and a payoff calculator.
- **Assets**: current and purchase value, appreciation metrics, a quick value-update dialog, and an **equity panel** when the asset is linked to its financing loan.
- **Investments**: portfolio summary, asset allocation, value chart, holdings, and income tracking.

## Investments & Portfolio

### Compare every security on one chart

The Security Performance report gains an **"All securities"** view that draws every active security's **cumulative percent return** on a single chart — each rebased to 0% at its first price, so holdings with different price levels and currencies share one axis. The comparison chart **exports to PDF**, and the report picked up account-type and text filters. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### See which securities drive your portfolio value

The Portfolio Value Over Time report and the dashboard investment chart can now toggle from a single total line to a **stacked per-security breakdown** — across daily, monthly, and intraday ranges — with smaller holdings rolled into an "other" band and cash shown as its own band, plus a tooltip that ranks each security's contribution.

### KEY:VALUE tags

Tags now understand a **`key:value`** naming convention (e.g. `country:poland`, `strategy:dividend`) — no migration, just a convention parsed from the tag name. On top of it: the Asset Allocation chart gains a **"By key"** mode that charts your portfolio's value-weighted split across a key's values, and **transactions can be filtered by tag key** (has value / no value / contains / doesn't contain), covering splits too. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### Geographic look-through

The Asset Allocation chart also gains a **"By country"** grouping that looks through your funds' holdings to show true country-level exposure — top 10 countries individually, the rest collapsed into "Other Countries". Allocation percentages now reconcile exactly against the drawn slices, and the legend layout was cleaned up.

## Transactions

### Search in your own format

The transactions search box now understands **amounts and dates typed the way you write them**. Paste `1 234,56` or `1234,56` in a comma-decimal locale, or type a date as it's displayed (`02.07.2026`), and Monize finds the matching transactions — on top of, never instead of, the existing text search. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### Quality of life

- **Filter by a payee or category** on the transactions page and an **info widget** summarizes it alongside the list, just like the account widget.
- A **year picker** in the calendar popover makes jumping to distant dates much faster.
- Monize now **warns before you edit or delete a reconciled transaction**, so a carefully balanced statement doesn't drift by accident.
- The Post Transaction modal got a cleaner layout and a **copy button on the amount**, and the split editor's columns line up better.

## AI Assistant

### Insights in your language, with links you can click

AI insights and forecasts are now **generated in your chosen language** — the model writes its prose in your locale while leaving numbers, currency codes, and proper nouns alone. Insight and chat text now **deep-links to the records it mentions**: transactions, payees, categories, securities, and scheduled bills open — and briefly flash — right where they live. After the assistant commits a change, list pages **refresh themselves immediately**, so you see the result without reloading.

### A better relay experience

For users who bring their own agent via the MCP relay: **insights and forecasts can now be generated through your connected agent** too, not just chat — with a live **relay status bar** on the Insights page showing the connection state. Relay chat also got a round of hardening from **@WMP**: confirmation cards now arrive reliably even on large, slow agent turns; conversation history sent to the agent is capped so long chats stay fast; attachments are detected by MIME type rather than file extension; and Unicode-bullet lists render as real lists.

### A movable chat panel

On desktop, the floating AI chat panel can now be **dragged anywhere on the screen** and remembers its position. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

## Languages

- **Default expense and income categories are now translated** across all 21 locales, so a fresh account starts in your language.
- **Every transactional email** is now localized to the recipient's stored language.
- Chart month markers now follow your language too.

## Bug Fixes

- Editing the counterpart leg of a **split transfer** no longer corrupts the split parent.
- **Backup restore now works for OIDC users**, re-authenticating through the live session instead of demanding an unobtainable ID token.
- Backups now include the **loan-scenario, rate-history, and security-tag tables**, so a restore brings your loan and tag data back too.
- **Auto-Merge Payees** now isolates a failing merge group instead of aborting the whole run, and guards against alias-pattern conflicts. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- Editing an account no longer **clears its institution** unless you explicitly clear it.
- Fixed a **portfolio value discrepancy** between daily and monthly ranges, and Asset Allocation percentages now always sum to 100% of the drawn slices.
- Investment account dropdowns are now **sorted alphabetically**.

## Under the Hood

- **TypeScript upgraded from 5.9 to 6.0.3** across the repo, as a bridge toward TypeScript 7.
- Ten new database migrations (`089`–`098`) covering dashboard widget preferences, loan scenarios, rate-change history, asset–loan links, and overpayment recognition settings.
- CI gained a **scheduled GHCR cleanup** job that prunes untagged image manifests.
- A date-dependent chart test now pins the clock. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
